Visualizing the COVID-19 Outbreak in the U.S.

The coronavirus outbreak started in the U.S. in late February/early March, 2020. This web page is a visualization of the evolutoin of the pandemic in the U.S. from the beginning of March, 2020. Total number of cases are shown by counties on the map. Additional details can be revealed by hovering your pointer over the map. Major U.S. cities and COVID-19 timeline are also annotated on the map. These annotations can be toggled on or off.

Several observations can be made by the interactive visualization. For example:

  • By moving the slider from the beginning to the end, it shows that the pandemic first hit major cities on the coasts (both west and east), then major cities inland, and finally less populated area in between.
  • By zooming in and hovering over counties to inspect time series in detail, it can be observed that some cities have succesfully flattened the curve (e.g. New York City) while others haven't.
  • By toggling on the marks of the major cities, it shows a strong correlation between case numbers and population.

    • You are encouraged to explore the map and come up with your own observations about the pandemic.

    Use slider bar to select a date; use scroll wheel or double-click to zoom in; hover over a county to show details.

    Source: Coronavirus (COVID-19) Data in the United States published by New York Times
