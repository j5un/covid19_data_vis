Visualizing the COVID-19 Outbreak in the U.S.

The coronavirus outbreak started in the U.S. in late February/early March, 2020. Here I present a visualization of the evolutoin of the COVID-19 pandemic in the U.S. The COVID-19 data is released by New York Time on GitHub, and contains cumulative data of both confirmed cases and deaths in the US. The second page presents the growth curve of the total COVID-19 cases in the US by time, while the third page contains an interactive choropleth map of detailed COVID-19 data by county. Additional details can be revealed by hovering your pointer over the map. Major U.S. cities and COVID-19 timeline are also annotated on both the line chart and the choropleth map.

Some observations can be readily made from the interactive visualization. For example:

  • While the total number of cases in the US seems to be accelerating, there is a lot variation among states. Some states that had early outbreaks seems to be flattening the curve (e.g. NY), which other states are seeing rapid growth rate.
  • By moving the slider bar from the beginning to the end on the choropleth map, it shows that the pandemic first hit major cities on the coasts (both west and east), then major cities inland, and finally less populated area in between.
